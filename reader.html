To get started with LastPass, create an account, install the LastPass browser extension, log in, and add data to your vault — like login credentials and passkeys — then start using LastPass to autofill and manage your passwords.

Step #1: Create an account

Create a LastPass account so you have a vault to store your passwords and other sensitive items.

  1. Go to lastpass.com and select Get LastPass Free — or Create an account.
  2. Enter your email address and select Sign up.
  3. Create your master password — the one password you’ll use to unlock your vault.
  4. Complete sign-up to create your vault.

Tip

Choose a strong, memorable master password. LastPass cannot reset it for you if it’s forgotten — set up account recovery after you finish these steps.

Step #2: Install the LastPass browser extension and log in

Install LastPass where you’ll use it most, then log in so LastPass can autofill on the sites you visit. Pick your platform below.

  1. Open the Chrome Web Store and search for LastPass.
  2. Select Add to Chrome, then confirm the permission prompt.
  3. Pin the LastPass icon to your toolbar.
  4. Select the LastPass icon and log in with your email and master password.
  1. From your vault, go to Account Settings and select Download.
  2. Run the installer for Windows or macOS and follow the prompts.
  3. Open the LastPass desktop app and log in with your email and master password.
  1. Install LastPass Password Manager from the App Store or Google Play.
  2. Open the app and log in with your email and master password.
  3. Enable autofill and biometric unlock when prompted.

Step #3: Add passwords and passkeys

Add login credentials and passkeys to your vault so LastPass can fill them in for you.

  1. When you sign in to a site, accept the LastPass prompt to save the password — or add it manually from your vault.
  2. Where a site supports passkeys, create or save a passkey with LastPass when prompted.
  3. Organize items into folders as needed so they’re easy to find later.

Passkeys

Passkeys are a faster, more secure replacement for passwords on supported sites and apps. Learn more in About passkeys.

Step #4: Use LastPass

With the extension installed and items in your vault, LastPass can autofill logins, generate strong passwords, and help you manage credentials across devices.

  • Visit a saved site and use LastPass autofill when the icon appears in the login fields.
  • Generate a strong password when you create a new account.
  • Open your vault anytime from the extension or lastpass.com to view, edit, or share items.
