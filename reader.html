LastPass stores your passwords and other sensitive information in a secure vault, then fills them in for you as you browse. This guide walks you through creating your account, installing LastPass, and adding your first items.

Your master password is the one password you need to remember.

Choose a strong, memorable passphrase — LastPass cannot reset it for you if it’s forgotten.

Before you begin

  • A supported browser — Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari — or a mobile device running iOS or Android.
  • An email address you can access to verify your account.
  • A few minutes to choose a strong, memorable master password.

Step 1 · Create your account

  1. Go to lastpass.com and select Get LastPass Free.
  2. Enter your email address and select Sign up.
  3. Create your master password — at least 12 characters mixing upper- and lower-case letters, numbers, and symbols.
  4. Select Sign up to create your vault.

Tip

Make your master password a passphrase — several unrelated words strung together are both strong and easy to recall.

Step 2 · Install LastPass

Install LastPass where you'll use it most. Pick your platform below.

  1. Open the Chrome Web Store and search for LastPass.
  2. Select Add to Chrome, then confirm the permission prompt.
  3. Pin the LastPass icon to your toolbar, then select it and log in with your master password.
  1. From your vault, go to Account Settings and select Download.
  2. Run the installer for Windows or macOS and follow the prompts.
  3. Open the LastPass desktop app and log in with your master password.
  1. Install LastPass Password Manager from the App Store or Google Play.
  2. Open the app and log in with your master password.
  3. Enable autofill and biometric unlock when prompted.

Step 3 · Add items to your vault

Your vault holds more than passwords. LastPass can securely store any of the following item types:

Item typeWhat it storesAutofills
PasswordSite username and passwordLogin forms
AddressName, street, city, postal codeCheckout & shipping forms
Payment cardCard number, expiry, security codePayment forms
Secure noteFree-form encrypted text—

Step 4 · Check your Security Dashboard

Open the Security Dashboard from your vault to see your overall security score, find weak or reused passwords, and turn on dark web monitoring so you're alerted if your email appears in a known breach.

Set up account recovery now

Configure at least one recovery option before you rely on LastPass. If you forget your master password, recovery is the only way back into your vault.
